Celebrating Dussehra

What is Dussehra?

Dussehra, also known as Vijayadashami, is an important Hindu festival that celebrates the victory of good over evil. It marks the end of Navaratri and is observed in different ways across India.

Significance

The festival commemorates the victory of Lord Rama over the demon king Ravana, symbolizing the triumph of righteousness. It also celebrates the goddess Durga's victory over the buffalo demon Mahishasura.

Celebrations

During Dussehra, effigies of Ravana, Meghnath, and Kumbhkaran are burned, signifying the destruction of evil. Various cultural events, including Ramlila performances, are organized, and people participate in prayers and feasting.

Regional Variations

In different regions, Dussehra is celebrated with unique customs. In the north, grand processions take place, while in the south, the festival is more focused on the worship of Durga.
